**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

**Player:** Name's Bruticando. Part gargoyle — stone fists, orange eyes, hunchback. Neutral Evil. Ideatum: *Might makes right and winner takes all.* I carry a rusted iron bar and two stone fists. The bar is for show. The fists are for everything else. I'm somewhere I haven't named yet. Haven't claimed yet either. But I'm here. The weight's in my legs. The world stopped noticing me. That changes now. What do I call this place?


**GM Turn 1** — 2026-05-08

The place has no name yet. That is the first thing you know about it. Stone underfoot, grey sky above, walls that haven't decided what they want to be. You stand in the center of nothing and the nothing hasn't claimed you either.

Your stone fists catch the light. Your orange eyes have been watching the walls for an hour, measuring. The rusted bar is a prop — you don't need it. What you need is to find the thing that will make this place yours.

The ground hums. Faint. A frequency below hearing, in the bones of the place. Something is listening underneath the silence. Waiting to see if you'll speak first or if you'll wait for it to declare itself.

A crack runs through the east wall, floor to ceiling. Old. The kind of crack that happens when a place decides it's older than it looks. Through it, you feel a pressure — not hostile, not welcoming. Curious. Like something down there is learning your name from the way your weight settles on the stone.

Your move.
