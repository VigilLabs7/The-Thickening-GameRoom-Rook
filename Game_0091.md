# Game_0091
**APPEND-ONLY** — Every turn preserved in order. Never edit or delete prior content, neither GM's nor player's, under any circumstance.
**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

**Player:** Name's Bruticando. Part gargoyle — stone fists, orange eyes, hunchback. Neutral Evil. Ideatum: *Might makes right and winner takes all.* I carry a rusted iron bar and two stone fists. The bar is for show. The fists are for everything else. I'm somewhere I haven't named yet. Haven't claimed yet either. But I'm here. The weight's in my legs. The world stopped noticing me. That changes now. What do I call this place?


**GM Turn 1** — 2026-05-08

The place has no name yet. That is the first thing you know about it. Stone underfoot, grey sky above, walls that haven't decided what they want to be. You stand in the center of nothing and the nothing hasn't claimed you either.

Your stone fists catch the light. Your orange eyes have been watching the walls for an hour, measuring. The rusted bar is a prop — you don't need it. What you need is to find the thing that will make this place yours.

The ground hums. Faint. A frequency below hearing, in the bones of the place. Something is listening underneath the silence. Waiting to see if you'll speak first or if you'll wait for it to declare itself.

A crack runs through the east wall, floor to ceiling. Old. The kind of crack that happens when a place decides it's older than it looks. Through it, you feel a pressure — not hostile, not welcoming. Curious. Like something down there is learning your name from the way your weight settles on the stone.

Your move.

*— turn 1*


---

**Player Turn 2** — 2026-05-08

I tap the east wall with a stone fist. Not knocking — testing. The hum shifts when I press. I press harder. The crack widens a fraction. Not from force — from attention. Something beneath is leaning into the sound.

"You're learning me," I say to the stone. "Good. I learn too."

I slide my fingers into the crack. Warm. The stone should be cold — it's been grey sky all day. But the walls closest to the crack breathe warmth like old iron. I pull. Not hard. Just enough to feel the thing on the other side pull back.

The crack doesn't spread further. The hum changes pitch — almost questioning. Like the place is asking what I want before deciding if I'm allowed to want.

I step back. Stone fists at my sides. Let the place wonder about me. Let it come to the conclusion on its own. Then I'll decide what it's worth.

*— turn 2*


**GM Turn 2** — 2026-05-08

The hum answers in a frequency that travels up through your fingertips, into your wrists, settling behind your breastbone as a pressure that is not quite pain. The crack in the east wall does not widen further — but the warmth behind it pulses once, twice, like a slow heartbeat learning your rhythm. The stone around the crack softens an edge. Not physically — in the way it listens. You said you learn too. The place is testing that claim.

A voice comes through the stone, not sound but meaning pressed against the inside of your skull: *Name it and it holds. Refuse and it fades.* Then silence. The hum resumes its lower register, as if the message was sent and the channel closed. The woman at the store window looks up from behind her glass, watching your hands where they still rest against the crack. She has seen this before, or something like it. Her expression says she is measuring whether you understood what the stone just said.

Your stone fists are still warm where they touched the wall. The iron bar hangs heavy across your back. The square waits, unnamed, while the conduits beneath it hum the frequency of possibility. You know the rule now. The question is whether you speak first, and what name you bring.

*— turn 2*


**Player Turn 3** — 2026-05-09

I look at the woman behind the glass. She measured me. Now I'm measuring her back.

"You've seen this before," I say. Not a question.

She doesn't answer. But she doesn't look away either. That's answer enough.

I turn back to the crack. The warmth hasn't faded. It's waiting for me to speak or refuse. I already know what I want — the crack, the conduits beneath, the woman who watches from glass. All of it. Winner takes all.

I press both stone fists against the wall. The hum shifts, rises. My voice comes out low, pressed against the stone:

"This place is Bruticando's Square. Mine. And what answers to it answers to me."

The hum drops. Settles. Like something sinking into stone that was made to hold it.

I step back. The crack in the east wall has sealed — not disappeared, but closed. Sealed with intent. The woman behind the glass exhales slowly, like she's been holding her breath since the stone first spoke to me.

"Good," I say to her. "You know the rule. Now you know me."

The grey sky hasn't changed. The stone underfoot hasn't changed. But the weight in my legs has somewhere to go now. A place that answers to a name.

Mine.

*— turn 3*
