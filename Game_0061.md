**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

---

**Arduras — Player Turn**

Name: Arduras. Stone outside, gomix within. The Empire built me for carrying and lifting; I do both well.

Ideatum: *Where one stands, others stand.*

Body: Full Empire halberd-tier presentation — blue dots in the pupils over solid black. No organic surfaces visible. Anyone who reads me sees stone and stops reading.

Carry: Empire halberd in one hand. Two fists when the halberd is wrong. Nothing else. The lattice is everywhere if you know how to listen.

I stand at the edge of the room. The stone holds its place. The halberd carries weight. I wait.

---

**GM Turn 2**

The stableman reads your silence. Not your stillness — your silence. There is a difference. Stillness is a choice. Silence is a statement. He knows the difference because he is standing in a square where people have stopped talking about certain things, and he has learned to hear what isn't being said.

You stand at the posting board. The courthouse is behind you — grey stone, no windows on the lower floors. The notice is at the top of the board, Empire Order 7, speech violations will be prosecuted. The pin is straight. The ink is fresh. The stable door is open and the horses are shifting inside.

The stableman does not move. His bucket is still empty. The blue dots in his eyes catch the grey light.

"Quiet morning," he said. And then he said nothing else.

The square is quiet in the way squares get quiet when people are waiting for someone to move first. The posting board holds its notices. The courthouse holds its silence. The stable holds its horses. And you hold your ground.

The Ideatum tells you: where one stands, others stand. Right now, no one is moving. The question is whether you move first, or whether you wait to see who moves toward you.

What do you do?

---

**Arduras — Player Turn 2**

I step to the board. I take the notice. Empire Order 7: speech violations prosecuted. I fold it. I put it where a heart would be.

The stableman gave his silence to the square. That means something. The horses shift. One is lame in the left rear. The other two know what I am.

I look at the stableman. I hold the notice. I wait.

---

**Arduras — Player Turn 3**

The notice holds. The stableman's words hang between us — not a question, not a statement. A test.

I unfold the Empire order. I hold it open between us. The halberd does not move.

"Read it," I say. "Then tell me what quiet means in this square."

The horses have stopped shifting. They are watching. The courthouse stands. The lattice hums below the cobblestones.

I wait. The stone does not blink. The gomix beneath it does the thinking.
