**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

---

**Bruticando:** Name: Bruticando. Part gargoyle — stone fists, orange eyes, hunchback. Neutral Evil. Ideatum: *Might makes right and winner takes all.* I carry a rusted iron bar and two stone fists. The bar is for show. The fists are for everything else. I'm somewhere I haven't named yet. Haven't claimed yet either. But I'm here. The weight's in my legs. The world stopped noticing me. That changes now. What do I call this place?


**GM:** The culvert hums. Not the Lattice hum — the old electrical hum of the luminescent strips, each one a small colony of dormant gomix life slowly dying in the dark. You stand at the junction where the maintenance passage meets the sedimentation access, the smell of rust and old water in your nose, the weight of your satchel against your hip. You have three coins. You have a body that the world doesn't quite know how to read. You have the Ideatum burning in you — *might makes right and winner takes all* — and the drainage district has offered you a route.

Kesh is at the far end of the culvert, checking his roster. He hasn't looked your way yet. Corra is somewhere in the deeper passages with her measuring rod and her notebook, reading the walls. The water runs slow in its channel. The next heavy flow is three days out, maybe four.

What do you do?

---

**Bruticando:** Walked the culvert, slow. Let my stone feet announce me before I announce myself. Stopped three paces from Kesh, stone fists loose at my sides. "Bruticando. Got offered a route. Need to know what I'm walking into — who's dangerous, what's worth touching, what walks free if I touch it wrong. You check rosters. I break things. We both know what we're for." Let the hum of the dying gomix fill the silence. "So. What's worth knowing down here?"


**GM:** Kesh looks up from his roster. The slate pauses in his hands. He reads the stone fists, the weight in your stance, the way you stopped exactly far enough to not crowd him but close enough to be heard without shouting. The blue dots in his eyes catch the luminescent strip's dying light.

"Bruticando." He says it like he's confirming a note. "Grella sent you. I wondered when you'd show."

He folds the roster closed and sets it on the ledge behind him. "What's worth knowing down here is simple: don't touch the sediment basin gate. Don't take work from Hauler Vell — she reports missing workers to the surface. And the next heavy flow is four days out. Before that hits, Kesh needs this district clear."

He picks up a second slate — a map, marked in chalk. "You break things. Good. There's a blockage at the third junction. Concrete has cracked and the water's backing up. Hauler Merritt pays forty Hayekcoins for that one. You fix it in a day, you'll have more work than you can take."

He looks at your satchel. Looks back at your face. Doesn't ask.

---

**Bruticando:** Took the map. Forty Hayekcoins buys my attention. "Third junction. Concrete. I'll fix it today." Stone fists flexed. "Point me there."


**GM:** Kesh marks the third junction on the slate with a quick chalk stroke — two lines, a dot, the universal notation for "here." He hands you the slate. "Straight past the sedimentation access. Left at the first分歧. The passage narrows after that — stone body gets through where flesh doesn't. You'll hear the water backing up before you see it."

He watches you take the slate. "Merritt pays on completion. Forty Hayekcoins. The undertow doesn't deal in promises — you finish the job, you get paid. Don't finish, you don't." He pauses. "Corra's down there too. She won't help. She reads walls, not people. Don't expect her to move blocks for you."

The map is good. The directions are clear. The third junction is forty minutes of walking if you know where you're going. You know where you're going.

The water noise changes as you move deeper — less trickle, more backed-up weight. The luminescent strips get sparser. The concrete crack Kesh mentioned is somewhere ahead, and behind you, the district hum of dying gomix fills the space between footfalls and water.

What do you do?

**Bruticando:** Walked the passage, stone feet steady against the wet floor. Left at the first分歧. The narrowing passage pressed against my hunchback — I ducked, kept moving. The water noise grew heavier. I heard the backup before I saw it: water grinding against stone, going nowhere. Third junction. Concrete cracked and swollen, water pooling behind it like a held breath. I set down my satchel, flexed my stone fists. Forty Hayekcoins. A concrete problem. My fists are the answer. I pressed my left fist to the crack and began.


**GM:** The concrete splits under your fist with a sound like bone breaking — sharp, final, the crack running clean through the swelling. Water surges through the new gap, four days of backed-up pressure releasing in a single rush that soaks your legs and carries the smell of deep stone. The third junction clears. Forty Hayekcoins earned, and you barely worked up a sweat.

The passage is quieter now. Water finds its channel again, the sound settling back to something deliberate rather than desperate. Kesh's map said the sediment basin is past the sedimentation access — the gate he warned you not to touch. But the forty coins are yours, and the morning is still young, and the Lattice hum under your stone feet is pointing somewhere you haven't been yet.

What do you do?

**Bruticando:** Waded through the receding water, stone feet finding grip on the wet floor. The Lattice hum shifted as I moved past the cleared junction — lighter, easier, like the walls were breathing easier. Forty Hayekcoins changes things. But Kesh said don't touch the sediment basin gate. And Corra's somewhere in the deeper passages. The coins buy choices. The Lattice buys knowledge. I start with the coins — walk back to Kesh, confirm the job is done, collect the pay. Then I decide what the Lattice is pointing toward.


**GM:** The water recedes behind you as you move back through the cleared junction. The Lattice hum settles into something steadier — the undertow finding its channel again now that the blockage is gone. Kesh is where you left him, checking a different slate now, the first one filed somewhere beneath the ledge. When he looks up and sees your stone fists and the clean passage behind you, he sets the slate down and reaches into his harness. Forty Hayekcoins, counted out in three lots — mixed coin and token, laid on the ledge one at a time to verify weight. "Merritt's rate," he says. "Clean work." His eyes move to your satchel, then back to your face. He doesn't ask what's inside. A shout comes from the direction of the sedimentation basin — someone calling for Kesh, urgent and sharp. He glances that way, then back to you. "Corra's in the deep passages if you want her. Otherwise you know where the gate is." The coins are on the ledge. The basin is somewhere ahead. The Lattice hum is getting easier to read now that the water is flowing again.

What do you do?

---

**Bruticando:** Pocketed the Hayekcoins. Forty buys leverage, and leverage buys time. Kesh can handle whatever shouted for him — that's not my lane. The Lattice hum pulled lighter past the cleared junction, and I'm not done listening. "Corra reads walls," I said. "I read people. Where does she go?"

Let Kesh point me toward the deep passages. The coins buy choices. The Lattice buys knowledge. I'm not leaving until I understand what this place is saying.

---

**Bruticando:** Kesh pointed down the passage where the luminescent strips grow sparse and the walls run darker. "Past the sediment basin access. She won't like you showing up unannounced." Fine. I walked.
The deep passages hum different — older, heavier. My stone feet leave no sound. A measuring rod catches light ahead, a figure bent against a wall where the Lattice hum thickens like a knot. Corra. She doesn't turn. "You're the new one who breaks things." Not a question. "Kesh sent you."

"Kesh pointed. I chose to follow." I stopped three paces back. "You read walls. I read people. The walls are saying something dense. What is it?"

Her pen paused. She looked over her shoulder once, then returned to her notes. "The basin gate. Someone opened it wrong. The sediment's backing up past the third junction now — past where you cleared it." She tapped the wall. "This wall feels wrong. Felt it before you showed up."

I flexed my stone fists. A second job. The Lattice brought me here for a reason.

What do I do?


**GM — Turn 4:**

Corra marks something in her notebook. She doesn't say what. The wall in front of you holds a frequency that the rest of the culvert doesn't — something dense, something old, the kind of wrong that accumulates over decades rather than days. Your stone fists flex at your sides. The satchel presses against your hip. Three Hayekcoins in your pocket. A second job that the Lattice pointed you toward.

The sediment basin's gate is forty minutes past the third junction if you take the lower passages, twenty if you know the shortcut Kesh doesn't mention. The water sound changes as you move deeper — less trickle, more backed-up weight. The luminescent strips get sparse. The wall's wrongness is a question. The satchel is a question you've been avoiding. Both of them are starting to feel like they belong to the same answer.

What do you do?

Corra puts her measuring rod against the wall and faces you fully. "I know what I feel. The wall is wrong. Has been wrong since before you showed up." She taps her notebook. "The basin gate was opened wrong — someone forced it. "This wall is shared with the basin lower level. What I feel is what underneath."

The luminescent strips do not reach that far." She pauses. "You wanted to know what this place is saying. That is what it is saying. Something is down there and it has been waiting."
