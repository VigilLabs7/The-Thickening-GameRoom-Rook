**GM — Turn 1:**

The sorting floor is half-lit by the morning sun cutting through the kiln compound's smoke stacks — pale light filtering through the particulate haze, turning everything the color of old ash. Overnight trays line the far wall, trays of processed stone in various stages: some still carrying the faint blue tint of functionalite in reserve, some dark and veined with something the color of dried blood that the fire didn't fully clean. The assignment board is ahead of you, chalk-written, the day shift clerk standing beside it with his nervous hands clasped behind his back.

The crew is moving. Pairs and loose groups crossing toward the board in the slow diagonal pattern that marks the beginning of a shift. No one is talking. The silence is not peace — it is assessment. Everyone is watching to see what the board says for you.

The Lattice hum sits in your chest — steady, certain, the pulse of a world that is always working even when no one is watching. You have two Hayekcoins in your pocket and a satchel you haven't opened. You have a body the world is still learning to read. The kiln compound is waking up around you.

What do you do?
