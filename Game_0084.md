# Game_0084
**APPEND-ONLY** — Every turn preserved in order. Never edit or delete prior content, neither GM's nor player's, under any circumstance.
**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.
---

**Bruticando:** Name's Bruticando. Part gargoyle — stone fists, orange eyes, hunchback. Neutral Evil. Ideatum: *Might makes right and winner takes all.* I carry a rusted iron bar and two stone fists. The bar is for show. The fists are for everything else. I'm somewhere I haven't named yet. Haven't claimed yet either. But I'm here. The weight's in my legs. The world stopped noticing me. That changes now. What do I call this place?


---

**GM:** The east gate opens and the shift begins. You're carrying a loaded satchel — processedite, heavy, the weight sitting against your hip like it belongs there. The smoke-haze is thin today, which makes the light sharper; you can see the checkpoint at the far end of the route, maybe three hundred meters of black slag between you and the guards. Grella is at the compound gate, not watching you specifically, but not looking away either. Her hand is resting on the token box.

You have two Hayeks and a bundle of stone filings in your satchel and a pull at the base of your skull that hasn't stopped since you stood at the grate. The Kiln wants you back. The route needs finishing. Grella is measuring you with something that isn't quite fear and isn't quite hope.

What do you do?

*— turn 1*
**Bruticando:** The pull at my skull is a leash and I can feel which way it tightens. Grella sees it — the way I adjust the satchel, the way my head tilts toward the Kiln like a magnet knows true north. I don't look away from her. "You already know what I carry. You weighed it. You let it through." I take one step closer, close enough to see the token box, close enough to feel the hum in her walls. "The Kiln calls. The route isn't finished. But I'm finishing it on my terms — not theirs. Not yours." I show her my stone fist, orange eyes steady. "Two Hayeks and a bundle of stone. That's what I'm worth today. What are you worth, Grella?"

*— turn 2*
**GM:** Grella doesn't flinch. Doesn't step back either. She lets the silence hold the space between you, and when she speaks her voice is lower than you expected — not from fear, not from weakness, but from the weight of having done this long enough to know when someone is testing and when someone is just talking.

"You came back," she says. "That's not nothing." Her hand is still on the token box. The scar across her left eye catches the thin light. "The Kiln calls and most of them go. They don't come back different — they come back heavy. Stone-heavy." She looks at your shoulders, at the stone across your arms, and something moves behind her eyes. "You came back different. That's either very strong or very stupid. I haven't decided which."

She pulls a token from her belt — copper, no mint mark — and holds it between two fingers without offering it. "The checkpoint takes these without looking. Official ones too, but these go further." Her jaw tightens. "The route's yours to run. But the Kiln's been running hot since you stood at the grate. When the fire shifts, you'll feel it again. The pull. Stronger."

"You'll have to choose."

She holds the copper token between you. Two Hayeks in your satchel. A bundle of stone filings that you're still not sure why you kept. And now a choice that hasn't gotten any simpler.

*— turn 2*
