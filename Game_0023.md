**GM Turn 2**

The evening market folds itself into night. Vendors close their stalls in silence, the way people handle things they do not want to drop. You stand at the edge of the crossing point — ten feet of open ground where the four roads stop short of meeting — and you feel the hum in your teeth. Four frequencies pressing together. And underneath them, something else.

The coin in your pocket is cold. You don't remember putting it there.

To your left, a woman with stone panels on her forearms watches you the way a locksmith watches a key she hasn't tried yet. She has been watching for ten minutes and she hasn't looked away.

To your right, a shape that is mostly air looks at you and then through you — the way someone looks at a frequency they are trying to tune.

And at the edge of the crossing point, where the open ground meets the thinning crowd, a shape that is not quite a person stands exactly where the four signals converge, and it is saying your name before you have decided whether to speak it.

What do you do?*
