That changes now. What do I call this place?
**GM Turn 1** — 2026-05-08T18:39:00Z

The afternoon is thinning toward evening. The crooked chimney sends a thin trail of smoke sideways in the still air. The shopkeeper inside has been watching you through the window — not hiding it, just watching. His hands do other things while he watches, muscle memory sorting stock, making change, the movements of a man who has done the same work ten thousand times. But his eyes stay on you.

You have the iron bar in both hands. You found it in an alley this morning and you have not set it down since. The rust comes off on your palms. The stone fists hold it steady.

The square is empty except for the two of you.

**What do you do?**



**GM Turn 2** — 2026-05-08T21:02:45Z

The shopkeeper has finished with the stock in his hands. He sets the last item down and his eyes come back to the window — to you. The flickering behind them stops, like a signal finding its frequency. He moves toward the door. The hinges groan. He steps out into the square with the iron bar in his sightline and he does not flinch.

"You been out here long," he says. Not a question. His hands hang at his sides. The blue dots in his eyes catch the last of the light. "The church door's been moving." He nods toward the dark steeple without looking at it. "That child knows something. Kids usually do."

He waits. The bar is heavy in your hands. The square is empty between you.
