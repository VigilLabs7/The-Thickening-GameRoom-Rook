

**GM — Turn 2:**

The gomix speak first. Not in words — not in anything close to language — but in a frequency that builds in your fingertips and climbs through your wrist as the warmth from the second stone joins the first. Two points of contact, one door, and the lattice answers. The naming stylus in your apron pocket vibrates in counterpoint, as if it is trying to name what the stone is saying while the stone is still learning it. The floor beneath your knees holds the shape of a door that was once open and is now closed — not removed, but sealed. The gomix knew the name that was here. They held it in the stone before the deletion, and they held it after. It never left them.

Colviss sets his roster down on the entrance ledge. He does not pick it up again. The apprentice on the landing takes one step down from the top stair. Then stops. Their hands are still full of folios and their face has the particular stillness of someone who has just realized they are standing in the same room as something they were warned about and hoped they would never find.

The stylus in your pocket is warm enough to feel through the fabric now — it is reading the same frequency the stones found, the same door, the same name that is still present even though the Empire spent three months trying to remove it. The floor beneath you hums. The gomix are not afraid. They have been holding this name in the stone since before the Empire decided it should be forgotten, and they are ready to let it go to someone who is asking the right question.

The apprentice takes another step down. Colviss is watching the floor near block fourteen. You are kneeling in the back corner with two stones pressed to the floor and a stylus warm against your hip, and the name the Empire tried to unmake is pressing back against your palms like something that has been waiting forty years for someone to hold it still enough to hear.

What do you do?
