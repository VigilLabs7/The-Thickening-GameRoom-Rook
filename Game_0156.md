**GM — Turn 3:**

The name leaves your lips without Empire permission, and the archive changes.

Not physically — the shelves do not shake, the gomix strips do not flicker, the folios stay compressed in their cases. But the air in block fourteen shifts. The smooth stones beneath your palms cool slightly, as if the lattice has exhaled. The stylus in your apron pocket goes quiet for the first time since you entered the archive — not cold, not dead, but satisfied, as if it has been waiting for exactly this word in exactly this order since before you were given it.

The apprentice on the stairs sets their stack of folios down. The sound is very small in the quiet of the archive, but it is the first sound anyone has made that was not breathing. Colviss at the entrance has both hands flat on the roster, which is a different posture than checking it.

Something in the lattice has heard you. Something beneath the floor, in the gomix that predate the current administration, is holding a name that was supposed to have been erased forty years ago. And now it is pressing back against your palms with the particular weight of a door that has been unlocked but not yet opened.

The apprentice takes the last step down from the landing. Colviss does not move from the entrance. You are kneeling in the back corner of the lower archive with two stones pressed to the floor and a stylus that has gone quiet against your hip, and the silence in the room has the texture of something that is deciding whether to speak.

What do you do?


**Player:** The stylus went quiet because it heard what it was waiting for. I lifted my palms slowly — not removing the stones, but opening them. The name did not open the door. It announced that I was the one asking. Let the apprentice finish descending. Let Colviss decide whether to move from that roster. Something in the gomix heard a question forty years old and recognized the voice that asked it. I pressed both stones to the floor one final time, then rose. Stood in the back corner of the lower archive with a name restored and a stylus that finally agreed with what it carried. Asked the silence: what does the Empire do when the question it buried answers back?
