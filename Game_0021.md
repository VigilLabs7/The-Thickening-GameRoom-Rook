# Game_0021

**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

**Player (Arduras):** Name: Arduras. Stone outside, gomix within. Ideatum: *Where one stands, others stand.* I carry an Empire halberd and two fists. I look stupid. Stone golems are not expected to think. I let it. I stand at the edge of the game. The halberd carries weight. I listen to what is not being said.
---

*GM Turn 1 — 2026-05-07*

The weight settles into your legs the way it always does when you've been standing long enough for the world to stop noticing you. The halberd is in your hands — Empire issue, the kind with the flared blade that catches light in ways that make it hard to see the edge. You've been carrying it since you arrived, and you've let the stone-dense quality of your frame do what it was designed to do: make you look like someone who isn't thinking.

The posting board is ten steps from where you're standing. The courthouse is behind it, grey stone, no windows on the lower floors. The woman on the residential road has finished her laundry and gone inside. The stableman is in the back, with the horses.

You move toward the board. Your boots are quiet on the cobblestones — you learned that in the Ocean, where sound carries strange and the space between arrivals is measured in breath rather than distance.

The notice is fresh. Someone pinned it this morning or last night. It reads:

**NOTICE: Speech violations will be prosecuted under Empire Order 7. Citizens are required to report suspected violations to the courthouse. Non-reporting is itself a violation.**

The ink is still dark. The pin is new.

You read it twice. The third time you read it, you count the letters in *required* and measure the angle of the courthouse roof against the morning sky and you feel the hum beneath your feet push back against the stone of your joints in a way that makes your left knee ache — the old wound, the one that never healed right.

The stableman comes around the corner of the stable with an empty bucket. He sees you reading the board. He sees the halberd in your hands and the way you're standing — weight settled, feet planted, the stance of someone who has decided not to be moved.

He pauses. The blue dots in his eyes catch the grey light.

"Quiet morning," he says. Not a question. Not a greeting. The kind of thing a man says when he's deciding whether to keep walking.

What do you do?

**Player (Arduras):** The stableman waits. His bucket is empty. The notice is fresh. I take my hand off the board.

"Waiting on the horses," I say. My voice grinds like gravel. People don't expect stone golems to speak. I use it. I stand where I stand. Let him decide what that means.
