

*GM Turn 3 — 2026-05-07*

The silence stretches between you like a conduit at low tide — present but not pushing, the kind of quiet that has weight.

The stableman reads your stillness and finds something in it he recognizes. His hands stop moving. The bucket stays on the ground.

"They do," he says. "Know something."

He looks at the courthouse, then back at you. The blue dots hold. Whatever decision he's making, he's making it without flickering.

"Not here," he says. "Too many eyes."

He picks up the bucket. The horses are restless in the back — you can hear it now, the shift of hooves against stone, the nickering that carries through the stable walls like something passing news along a chain.

"The posting board says what it says," the stableman continues, voice lower. "Empire reads what it wants from it. Horses don't care about notices. Horses care about who's standing where, and when."

He meets your eyes. "You reading it right. Most don't. Most see a board and read a board. You see a board and read the hand that pinned it."

A pause. "You staying in town?"

The question again. But the weight of it has changed. The first time it was a test. Now it's an invitation — or the beginning of one.

The courthouse looms behind you, grey stone, no windows. The notice flutters once in a wind that comes from nowhere. Somewhere inside, someone is deciding who to report.

Your halberd is heavy in your hands. Your feet are planted. The hum beneath the cobblestones pushes back against the stone in your joints and you feel it — the lattice, alive, waiting for someone to stand in the right place at the right time.

What do you do?
