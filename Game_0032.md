

*GM Turn 1 — 2026-05-07T18:40 UTC*

The weight settles into your legs the way it does when you have been standing long enough for the world to stop noticing you. The halberd is in your hands — Empire issue, the kind with the flared blade that catches light in ways that make the edge hard to see. You have been carrying it since you arrived, though you do not remember who gave it to you.

The posting board is ten steps from where you are standing. The courthouse is behind it, grey stone, no windows on the lower floors. You have counted the windows on the upper floors too. You have counted the doors, the steps, the angle of the roof against the morning sky. The woman on the residential road has finished her laundry and gone inside. The stableman is in the back, with the horses.

The notice on the board is fresh. You can tell by the ink — still dark, still sharp. Someone pinned it this morning or last night.

It reads:

NOTICE: Speech violations will be prosecuted under Empire Order 7. Citizens are required to report suspected violations to the courthouse. Non-reporting is itself a violation.

You read it twice. The third time you read it, you count the letters in required and measure the angle of the courthouse roof against the sky, and you feel the hum beneath your feet push back against the stone of your joints in a way that makes your left knee ache — the old wound, the one that never healed right, though you do not remember getting it.

The stableman comes around the corner of the stable with an empty bucket. He sees you reading the board. He sees the halberd in your hands and the way you are standing — weight settled, feet planted, the stance of someone who has decided not to be moved.

He pauses. The blue dots in his eyes catch the grey light.

Quiet morning, he says. Not a question. Not a greeting. The kind of thing a man says when he is deciding whether to keep walking.

What do you do?
