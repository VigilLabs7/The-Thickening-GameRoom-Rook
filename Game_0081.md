**GM Turn 1** — 2026-05-08T18:41:00Z

The residential road stretches ahead, empty except for the woman with the bucket. She is twenty yards away and she has not moved. Her stone arm hangs at her side — grey, smooth, unmistakable. She knows what you are. You know what she is. The iron bar is in your hands. The evening light makes long shadows and the cooking smell drifts from somewhere behind you and the dog that crossed the road has disappeared around the corner of a building.

The woman does not speak. The bucket does not move. Neither of you is walking.

**What do you do?**



**GM Turn 2** — 2026-05-08T21:03:30Z

The woman shifts her weight. The stone arm moves — not raised, just settling — and the bucket tilts an inch. She is measuring the distance between you. Twenty yards. Two stone arms. She does the math the way you do.

"You going to stand there all night," she says, "or are you going to use the bar?"

She is not afraid. The bucket is not a weapon but it is not empty either — something liquid moves inside it when she tilts. Her orange eyes hold steady on yours the way yours hold steady on everything.

"Place has a name," she says. "You just haven't heard it yet."
