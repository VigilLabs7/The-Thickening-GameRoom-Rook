

*GM turn 3 — 2026-05-07*

You take your time. The corner man is the first read — the one with the mug and the stare that doesn't blink. He's been here long enough to wear a groove in that chair. His clothes are plain but clean, and there's a tell in the way he angles his body: always facing the door. He is watching the room the way a predator watches a water hole. Not hungry. Not full. Just present, and patient, and waiting for something specific to arrive.

The bouncer is another matter. Broad stone shoulders under patched leather. His hands are stone fists set into arms that don't need to move to be intimidating. He stands near the stairs like a question the tavern is not ready to answer. His eyes track you when you move and return to the middle distance when you stop. He is not part of the room's economy — he is enforcement.

The menu tells its own story. Stale bread. Watered wine. Cold stew. The prices are written in a hand that has stopped caring. Someone is running this place on the minimum necessary to stay open.

The woman behind the bar is the one who knows things. The scar climbing her jaw says she has been on both sides of trouble, and the way she doesn't look up when you walk in says she has learned what is worth seeing and what isn't. Her hands move in slow circles on the glass. She is listening to the room the way the corner man watches the door — with the patience of someone who has made a decision about what kind of trouble is worth engaging.

A worker at the bar mutters something to the woman. She shakes her head. The man goes back to his drink.

You have the room's shape now. The corner man is a watcher. The bouncer is a wall. The barkeep is a door — if you know how to knock.

What do you do?
