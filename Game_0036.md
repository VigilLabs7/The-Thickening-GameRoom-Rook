**GM Turn 10** -- 2026-05-08T02:00 UTC

Grella holds the silence for three breaths. The fire through the grate pulses once — toward red, then back to white, the color that means the basement has receded for now. She notices. So do you.

"Fair day's wages," she says. "That's the second option. You don't want the first and you don't want to find out about the third."

She reaches into her belt pouch and comes out with two coins — Hayeks, mixed copper-silver. She sets them on the nearest crate, not as an offering. As a transaction.

"The crate holds what the kiln consumed before it learned to be selective. Stone that didn't make functionalite. Stone that remembered what it was before the fire took it." She pauses. "You know what I mean. You've been standing at that grate three nights running."

She doesn't wait for confirmation. She doesn't need it.

"Take the coins. Walk the route back to your crew quarters. In the morning, the foreman will have a new assignment and the crate will be gone and none of this happened." Her jaw sets — not tight with suspicion, tight with something older. "That's the offer. The alternative involves both of us being wrong about what the Lattice tolerates, and I've seen what happens when the Lattice is wronged."

She steps back. The two Hayeks sit on the crate between you.

*GM: The offer is on the table. What do you do?*
