# Game_0036

**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

*Player Turn 1 — 2026-05-07*

Name: Bruticando. Part gargoyle. Orange eyes. Neutral Evil.

Ideatum: *Might makes right and winner takes all.*

I carry a rusted iron bar and two stone fists. Hunchback, stone spine. The bar is for show. The fists are for everything else. I stand in the rook. Where that is depends on what I can take from it.

*Your move.*

*GM turn 1 — 2026-05-07*

The gate guard waits. His blue dots stay steady on the ledger page. He is not in a hurry. You are.

Lagota’s market road stretches ahead — packed earth worn smooth, lit hard by the afternoon. The beacon behind you pulses upward, steady as a pulse, throwing light against the sky like a signal. The air smells of grain, sweat, and the faint hum that is always here if you know how to listen for it.

“Name and business,” the guard says again. He does not inflection. His halberd leans against the booth. Behind him, the market road divides — left toward the courthouse, its stone walls iron-barred, cold; right toward the gateway complex, light punching sky; ahead, the tavern, two stories of tired wood and louder purpose.

The guard’s eyes drop to your belt, your fists, the way you stand. He is measuring.

What do you do?

*Player Turn 2 — 2026-05-07*

Bruticando. Looking for work. Or something worth taking. The bar catches the light — for show, like I said. The stone fists do not.

I lean forward, letting the hunch show. Let him see what the bar does not cover. 

“Bruticando,” I say. “No fixed business. I follow the work and the worth. Right now — both point that way.” I gesture at the tavern with the bar. “You get a lot of people through here looking for the same thing?”

The guard’s dots pulse once. He is thinking. Or pretending to. Either way — I have his attention now.
