**GM Turn 1:**

The weight settles into your legs the way it always does when you've been standing long enough for the world to stop noticing you. The halberd is in your hands — Empire issue, the kind with the flared blade that catches light in ways that make it hard to see the edge. The posting board is ten steps ahead. The courthouse is behind you, grey stone, no windows on the lower floors. The stable door is open. The horses are shifting inside.

The stableman comes around the corner with an empty bucket. He sees the halberd, the stance, the way you've planted your weight. He pauses. The blue dots in his eyes catch the grey light.

"Quiet morning," he says. Not a question. Not a greeting. The kind of thing a man says when he's deciding something.

What do you do?
