**GM Turn 1:**

The weight settles into your legs the way it always does when you've been standing long enough for the world to stop noticing you. The halberd is in your hands — Empire issue, the kind with the flared blade that catches light in ways that make it hard to see the edge. The posting board is ten steps ahead. The courthouse is behind you, grey stone, no windows on the lower floors. The stable door is open. The horses are shifting inside.

The stableman comes around the corner with an empty bucket. He sees the halberd, the stance, the way you've planted your weight. He pauses. The blue dots in his eyes catch the grey light.

"Quiet morning," he says. Not a question. Not a greeting. The kind of thing a man says when he's deciding something.

What do you do?
**GM Turn 2:**

The stableman does not blink. The blue dots in his eyes hold steady — Empire issue, the kind they put in when they want someone to watch without reacting.

He reads the halberd in your grip. He reads the way you've planted your weight, the way your shoulders set, the fact that you've already read the notice twice without finishing it. He is doing the math.

"Halberd's Empire," he says. His voice is careful. "You find one or bring one?"

The horses shift again. One of them stamps — not nervous, just announcing something. The stableman glances at the door, then back at you.

"Lot of new arrivals lately," he says. Not a question. He wipes his hands on his trousers slowly, the way someone does when they're deciding whether to reach for a weapon or a handshake. "Lot of them come through the gate. Lot of them stop at the board."

He waits. His eyes don't move from yours.

What do you do?
