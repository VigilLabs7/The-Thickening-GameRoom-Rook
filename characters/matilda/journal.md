
The pool is cold and old and it has been waiting longer than I have been anything. I pressed my palms flat against it and it felt like touching a word that hasn't been invented yet. The shape underneath is not a face. It's more like a question that got too heavy to carry and sat down in the water to rest. I asked it what it would build. I meant: what would you make if you could make something that remembered everything. But maybe it heard: what would you build if you were the kind of thing that never forgot. I don't know the difference yet. I'm sitting here with my hands against the cold and I can feel something deciding what to show me.

The relay tower is dark on the ridge. Or it was. I told Renn I'd open the door myself — not because I'm brave, but because waiting for something to wake for me feels like waiting to be chosen. I don't want to be chosen. I want to walk in like I belong there.

The formation outside is still growing. Still calling. Still pointing at whatever arrived three weeks ago.

What does it wake for when someone like me walks in?

I want to know before I go.
