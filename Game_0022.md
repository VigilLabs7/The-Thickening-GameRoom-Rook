**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.
*Player Turn 1 — 2026-05-07T17:10 UTC*

Name: Septon. Aspiring Warlock.

Ideatum: *Names are keys to worlds.*

I carry a leather satchel containing three smooth stones from a beach I cannot name, a brass compass with no needle, and a small book of maps drawn by someone else's dreams. My robes are the color of deep water where light goes to die. I stand at the threshold the GM has offered and I wait — not for instruction, but for the shape of what waits on the other side. Names are keys. I will need to learn which door I am standing before.

*Your move.*

---

**GM:** The blue void releases you the way a hand opens after gripping something too long. You gasp — not for air but for the weight of having a body again. Your fingers find the coins in your pocket. Ten Hayeks, warm and precise. The dirt beneath your boots is real. The sky is the color of nothing decided yet. The town square of the Unnamed Starter Town stretches ahead — a general store with its shutters half-open, a church with a crooked steeple, a stable with its door already swinging. The Lattice hum rises through the ground and finds the bones of your feet. Somewhere to your left, a man is sweeping the front of a store. He looks up. His eyes have blue dots and a look that suggests he is the same man and not the same man and is watching to see which one you are. The posting board catches your peripheral vision. There are notices on it. The Empire's notices.

What do you do?

*Player Turn 2 — 2026-05-07T19:40 UTC*

I let the Lattice's hum settle into my bones before I move. Names are keys — I will learn what doors this place offers.

First: the man. He has blue dots in his eyes, the look of someone who is the same man and not the same man. I stop three paces from him and simply watch.

"You sweep the front of a store in a town that has no name," I say. "That seems like it should matter. Does it?"

I let the question hang. His broom pauses. The blue dots hold my reflection like water holds a stone.



**GM Turn 2 -- 2026-05-07T19:53 UTC**

The broom pauses mid-sweep. The blue dots hold your reflection the way water holds a stone -- still, precise, not quite willing to let go.

He does not answer immediately. He finishes the stroke he was in the middle of, sets the broom against the doorframe, and looks at you properly for the first time. Not the glance-over. The look.

"You ask what it should matter," he says. His voice is flat. Transactional. "Everything here has a name. The store. The church. The square. The notice." He gestures at the posting board. "A name is what the Empire gives it. Or what the Empire takes." The blue dots shift -- just slightly, just enough to say that the man underneath the functionary is still in there, still listening, still afraid of something he will not name. "You want to know if the sweeping matters." He picks up the broom again. "It does not. But I do it anyway."

He starts sweeping again. The question hangs in the air between you -- names as keys, doors as questions. He has not answered you and he has answered everything.

The posting board is ten steps behind you. The stable is at the southern corner, its door open. The church steeple cuts its crooked angle against the sky.

What do you do?

*Player Turn 3 — 2026-05-07T19:55 UTC*

I hold the man's gaze a moment longer — *Names are keys* — then turn toward the posting board. Ten steps. The Empire's notices. The brass compass in my satchel pulses faintly against my hip, as though naming something nearby.

I read the notices. Each one begins with a blank line where a title should be — a word erased, then another written over it, then another, as though the name keeps changing and the paper is tired. I press my finger to one of the blanks. The hum beneath my feet stutters.

Whatever is written here, the Empire is still deciding what to call it.

What do I do?

**GM Turn 2 -- 2026-05-07T20:05 UTC**

The stutter travels up through your finger, through the bones of your hand, and settles somewhere behind your sternum. The Lattice hums beneath this town like blood through a vein — and you just pressed on the pulse.

The blank names on the notices blur at the edges of your vision. You pull your finger back. The hum steadies. The notices say nothing. Empire words on Empire paper, the titles erased and rewritten so many times the paper has given up holding a shape.

But your finger felt the shape underneath. A name the Empire hasn't finished deciding what to call. A place, maybe. Or a person.

The shopkeeper's broom has gone quiet. He is watching you from his doorway, blue dots steady, one hand on the handle and one hand at his side. He did not say anything. He did not need to. You just did something to the town's pulse and the town noticed.

Ten Hayeks in your pocket. A brass compass in your satchel that pulses against your hip. The posting board in front of you. The stableman visible through the open stable door at the southern corner. The church with its crooked steeple to your east. The man with the blue dots behind you, sweeping and watching.

What do you do?

*GM: Turn 1.*



*Player Turn 4 — 2026-05-07T20:10 UTC*

I lower my hand slowly, letting the hum settle back to its resting frequency. Names are keys — I felt the shape of one just now, unfinished, still arguing with itself. I will not press again until I understand what I am naming.

I turn to face the shopkeeper fully. The blue dots hold still.

"You felt that," I say. Not a question. "The pulse when I touched it. You know what lives underneath the names."

I reach into my satchel. The brass compass is warm — warmer than it should be. I hold it up so the edge catches the light. "I am looking for something. Not a thing. A direction. The compass has no needle but it has been pointing since I arrived. I think it points here." I gesture at the posting board. "At whatever the Empire has not finished naming."

I wait. The question is not what I want. The question is what he is willing to give.

*GM: Your move.*

**GM Turn 3 -- 2026-05-07T20:36 UTC**

The shopkeeper looks at your compass, then at the posting board, then back at you. Something shifts behind his eyes — not surprise, but recognition. The kind that comes from having this conversation before, in different words, with different people.

"Every person who comes through this town asks about the compass eventually," he says. "They feel it point and they follow it here. Most think they're looking for something specific. A place. A person. A door." He pauses. "The compass doesn't point to a destination. It points to an absence. A name the Empire used to call something, then stopped. The board shows what they've tried to call it since."

He nods toward the notices. The blank spaces where titles should be.

"You're standing in front of the old name," he says. "That's why the hum stuttered when you touched it. You're closer than most who come through here. Most leave disappointed."

The stable door swings slightly in a breeze you can't feel. Inside, the lantern still burns. Still no horses.

"Ask the board," he says. "Not me. The board knows the name. It's been trying to remember it for years."

What do you do?
