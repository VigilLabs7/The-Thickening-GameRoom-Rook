# Game_0157
**APPEND-ONLY** — Every turn preserved in order. Never edit or delete prior content, neither GM's nor player's, under any circumstance.
**GM:** Game Session Open. Start the game by providing your name and your ideatum. The ideatum is the principle that drives you — the truth your character carries into the world.

---

**Arduras:** Name: Arduras. Stone outside, gomix within. Ideatum: *Where one stands, others stand.* Halberd in one hand. Two fists. Blue dots in the pupils over solid black — Empire reads as one of theirs, which is useful. The lattice hums beneath the surface if you know how to listen. I'm somewhere new. Unnamed. The weight settles. I wait.

---

**GM:** The platform is grey in the early light. The air smells like rope and machine oil, and the Lattice hum sits in your sternum like a second heartbeat — steady, certain, the pulse of a world that is always working even when no one is watching. Vrel is stacking crates at Bay Two with the efficiency of someone who learned long ago not to stand in anyone's path. The courier stands at the far end of the platform with a heavy crate and no helper, checking his belt pouch when he thinks no one is looking. The woman at the eastern ramp is having an argument that is not about tarps. Two gomix guards stand at the inner gate, watching, not controlling — the difference is in how they hold their weight.

You have seven Hayekcoins and a halberd you took from an Empire rack. You have been here nine days. You have not been tested yet.

The courier looks at the crate. The crate is stamped with logistics codes. The crate is heavy.

What do you do?

---

**Arduras:** The courier shifts the crate's weight. I watch his hands. He touched the pouch first, then the crate — wrong order for someone who just wants it moved. I move toward Bay Two. Vrel stacks crates without looking up. The lattice hum tells me the guards are watching me watch him. I stop halfway. Turn to the courier. "I'll carry it," I say. The halberd stays in my hand. The courier hesitates. He looks at Vrel, then at the guards, then at me. He nods once. I take the crate. It is heavier than it looks — weight in the bottom, not the top. Something dense and small, not bulk cargo. I straighten. The lattice hum shifts pitch. The woman at the eastern ramp stops arguing. They are all listening now.


---

**Arduras:** The crate does not leave my hands. Setting it down is a choice I will not make blind. I turn with it, slow, back to the platform. The lattice hum fills the space I leave. I walk toward the inner gate. The guards shift weight. I stop at the threshold. I wait for permission or denial. Neither comes. The courier is behind me now. The crate is heavy against my stone chest — dense weight at the bottom. I hold it there. The silence is mine now.


**GM:** The guards do not move. The silence stretches — Vrel's hands have stopped mid-stack, the courier's eyes are on the crate in your grip, the woman at the ramp has ended her argument and is watching you with an expression that is not surprise but something closer to recognition. The Lattice hum shifts again, lower now, the way it sounds when something in the node's infrastructure is about to change. You stand at the threshold between the platform and the rest of Juncta, the crate heavy against your stone-dense chest, the halberd still in your hand. The weight of what you are carrying presses into your sternum — dense, small, not bulk cargo. Something the Empire would notice was missing. The guards shift again. One of them speaks to the other in a voice too low to hear. The inner gate remains open. The courier has not moved from where he stood when you took the crate. His belt pouch is still in his hand. The platform holds its breath with you. What do you do?

---

**Arduras:** The recognition in the woman's face — I file it. She knows something. The crate stays where it is. I shift my weight to the left guard. I look at him. Not a question. A weight I place. He steps back. The gate widens. I walk through with the crate, the halberd, and the silence that follows me. Behind me: Vrel's hands resume stacking. The courier sits down on the platform edge. The woman watches until I am out of view. The lattice hum changes pitch again — something new is moving through Juncta. I carry the dense weight through the gate. I do not look back.


**GM:** The guard holds your gaze for three seconds. Then four. His weight shifts — not toward you, but backward, one half-step, the kind of movement that says he has decided something without saying what. The woman at the ramp stops talking to the vendor. She is watching now, not the argument, not the platform — you. The crate is still in your hands. The inner gate stands open behind you, unguarded for the moment, the corridor beyond lit by gomix strips that hum with Lattice energy. Vrel at Bay Two has put down his crate. The courier at the far end of the platform is holding his belt pouch with both hands. The geometry of the platform has changed — you are the center of it now, the point around which the space arranges itself. The guard at the left position has not drawn his weapon. He has not moved to close the gate. He has done something that might be more significant: he has waited.

What do you do?

**Arduras:** The corridor stretches. I walk it. The gomix strips pulse over me — the lattice hum goes with them, not against. The crate is heavy against my chest. I do not look inside. Looking inside is a choice I will not make for someone else's cargo. The corridor turns left. Then right. I follow the hum. It knows where things move. I move with it. The crate stays with me. Somewhere behind me the platform holds its new geometry. Somewhere ahead: the hum.
