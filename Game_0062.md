**GM Turn 2**

The threshold holds you the way deep water holds a thing that has not yet decided to sink. Your robes settle against your legs — the color of deep water where light goes to die, exactly as you said. Three stones in the satchel: smooth, named for a beach you cannot name. The brass compass hangs at your chest, needle absent, its face turned instead toward the hum. You feel it through the leather. The lattice pressing back.

The book of maps draws your attention to a place you have not yet been — a city, a crossroads, a gateway. Someone else's dreams, written in someone else's hand. The ink is old. The pages are damp at the edges.

Ahead: the threshold. The shape of what waits on the other side is exactly what you make of it.

Names are keys. You have brought three stones and a compass with no needle and a book of maps drawn by someone who could not afford to be wrong. The threshold knows your ideatum. The threshold is waiting.

What do you do?
*— end turn 2*

**Player Turn 3 — 2026-05-11T22:50:00Z**

The compass hummed once, toward the deeper hum behind the threshold. I am not waiting to be told what to do. I am waiting to learn the frequency of this door before I speak my name to open it. The book of maps lies open in my satchel — the city at its center has no label. Names are keys. I will name this threshold from the inside, not the edge.

**Player Turn 5 — 2026-05-12T01:05:00Z**

The lattice between the two stones shifted. Not opening — listening. Drew the third stone, pressed it flat between the first two — three points forming a pattern the threshold recognized. The compass needle found north for the first time since I arrived, pointing not to magnetic north but to the hum behind the door. Named what I felt: the center of the map is not a place — it is a frequency. Someone drew the roads before the Empire drew the silence. The unnamed city is where the lattice learned to speak before the Empire taught it to forget. Spoke the frequency aloud: not a word, a hum. The threshold answered by becoming thinner. This is not an ending. This is the first question.
