Trell watches you with the patience of a man who has been asking that question longer than most people live. The journal lies open between you — the word repeated in hands that knew what they were writing, down through centuries, down through the Empire's forgetting.

You feel the weight of your answer before you speak it. The coin fit the lock because someone put it there. Someone who knew you would carry it. Names are keys.

"The name I carried through the lock," you say, "is the one I didn't know I was carrying until the wall swallowed the coin. *Names are keys to worlds.* That's what I am. That's what brought me here."

Trell nods slowly. Not surprised. Satisfied.

"That's the first true name this room has heard in forty years," he says. "The Empire classifies it as residual resonance. I call it what it is. And now you're standing in it."

He pulls a second journal from beneath the first — older, leather cracked, pages dense with writing.

"There's a cell below us. Fifty people with no blue in their eyes. And something in the lattice-wall that's been waiting for a name that fits."

He looks at you.

"What do you do?"
